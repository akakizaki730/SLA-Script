Resolving build chamber RTD timeouts for the Fuse 1

You must insert  a build chamber into the Fuse 1 before a print can begin. The Fuse 1 measures the temperature of the build chamber with a Resistance Temperature Detector (RTD) sensor located within the build chamber. If the build chamber RTD cannot accurately give temperature readings, the print does not proceed. Firmware issues, faulty pin connections, and unseated cables can cause this error.

Use this guide to resolve RTD timeouts on a Fuse 1 build chamber.

Required supplies:

  • Compressed air
  • DeOxit or equivalent contact cleaner / corrosion preventative compound
  • Multimeter
  • 2.5 mm hex driver
  • Antistatic wrist band
  • Nitrile gloves

Estimated time: 45 minutes–1 hour

Preparing the workspace

OVERVIEW: Prepare the build chamber and a dust-free workspace for cleaning the build chamber socket and plug.

STEP 1: PREPARE THE WORKSPACE

Clear a workspace around your build chamber. Ensure that the workspace is well-ventilated and free of dust.

STEP 2: TURN OFF THE PRINTER

Disconnect the power cable before continuing with maintenance. Do not perform maintenance on the printer while it is connected to power. Wait five minutes after disconnecting the printer from power before proceeding.

DANGER: Performing maintenance on the printer while it is plugged in increases the risk of electric shock. Never remove the back panel of the printer while the printer is plugged in. Never reconnect the printer to power while the back panel is uninstalled.

Cleaning the build chamber socket and plug

OVERVIEW: Remove any debris from the build chamber socket and plug.

STEP 1: DISCONNECT AND REMOVE THE BUILD CHAMBER

Disconnect the build chamber from the printer. Remove the build chamber and place on a clean, horizontal surface.

STEP 2: CLEAN THE BUILD CHAMBER SOCKET AND PLUG

Use compressed air to clean out the build chamber plug and the adjacent socket, located in the lower-right hand corner of the printer, next to the print enclosure.

STEP 3: APPLY CONTACT CLEANER TO THE BUILD CHAMBER PLUG AND SOCKET

Apply contact cleaner to the build chamber plug and to the socket on the front of the printer. Allow the unit to sit for an hour until the contact cleaner dries sufficiently.

STEP 4: RELOAD THE BUILD CHAMBER

Reload the build chamber into the printer. Reinsert the build chamber plug into the socket.

STEP 5: START A PRINT

Start a print. If the printer continues to show RTD errors, proceed with the following instructions.

Testing the resistance of the build chamber temperature sensor

OVERVIEW: Test the resistance of the build chamber temperature sensor pins to ensure that they have not been damaged.

STEP 1: TURN OFF THE PRINTER

Disconnect the power cable before continuing with maintenance. Do not perform maintenance on the printer while it is connected to power. Wait five minutes after disconnecting the printer from power before proceeding.

DANGER: Performing maintenance on the printer while it is plugged in increases the risk of electric shock. Never remove the back panel of the printer while the printer is plugged in. Never reconnect the printer to power while the back panel is uninstalled.

STEP 2: REMOVE THE BUILD CHAMBER

Disconnect the build chamber from the printer. Remove the build chamber and place on a clean, horizontal surface.

STEP 3: SET THE MULTIMETER

Set the multimeter to check circuit resistance. This is typically labeled “Resistance,” “Ohms,” or “Ω” on most multimeters.

STEP 4: CONNECT THE MULTIMETER TO THE TEST LEADS

Connect the black test lead to the jack marked "Common" or "-". Connect the red test lead to the jack marked with the “Ω” or letter "R".

TIP:  Zero out your multimeter before testing build chamber plug pins to ensure accurate results. Touch the test probes at the end of the test leads together and rotate the “Zero Adjust” knob until the meter reads 0.

STEP 5: UNHOLSTER THE BUILD CHAMBER PLUG

Unholster the build chamber plug from the rubber retainer so you can see the face of the plug.

STEP 6: LOCATE THE BUILD CHAMBER PLUG PINS

Locate pins 9, 10, 11, and 12. These pins are the most likely to throw RTD errors.

STEP 7: TEST THE RESISTANCE OF THE BUILD CHAMBER PLUG PINS

Use the multimeter to test the build chamber temperature sensor pins:

  • Place one test probe against Pin 9 and the second test probe against Pin 10. The multimeter should read around 0 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 10 to Pin 11. The multimeter should read around 1100 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 11 to Pin 12. The multimeter should read around 0 Ohms.

TIP:  As long as your printer is unplugged, there is no difference between the red and black test leads.

Please take note of the readings for the three test points listed above, and contact Formlabs Support or your certified service provider if any do not match the expected readings.

STEP 8: TEST THE RESISTANCE OF BUILD CHAMBER PLUG PINS, CONT.

If the readings for pins 9, 10, 11, and 12 are correct, check the following pins:

  • Test Pin 13 to Pin 14. The multimeter should read around 0 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 14 to Pin 15. The multimeter should read around 1100 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 15 to Pin 22. The multimeter should read around 0 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 23 to Pin 24. The multimeter should read around 0 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 24 to Pin 25. The multimeter should read around 1100 Ohms.
  • Test Pin 25 to Pin 26. The multimeter should read around 0 Ohms.

Please take note of the readings for the six additional test points listed above, and contact Formlabs Support or your certified service provider if any do not match the expected readings.

Reseating the build chamber connection cable

OVERVIEW: Reseat the build chamber connection cable to the temperature board.

DANGER: The Fuse 1 contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using a grounding strap or touching a grounded piece of metal.

STEP 1: UNPLUG CONNECTIVITY ACCESSORIES

Uninstall the Wi-Fi antenna. Unplug the USB and Ethernet cables from the bottom right rear of the printer. Set aside for later.

STEP 2: UNSCREW THE LOWER REAR PANEL

Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the six black cap screws and four chrome cap screws securing the lower rear panel to the printer. Set the screws aside for later use.

NOTICE: The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image above for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.

STEP 3: REMOVE THE LOWER REAR PANEL

Remove the lower rear panel. Lay the lower rear panel down on a flat, clean surface.

STEP 4: GROUND YOURSELF

DANGER: The Fuse 1 contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using a grounding strap or touching a grounded piece of metal.

Put on an anti-static wrist band and establish a valid grounding point before touching any of the internal boards or cables.         

STEP 4: LOCATE THE TEMPERATURE BOARD

With the panel removed and set aside, locate the temperature board at the center-left of the printer. It is located directly above the fan and labeled “MoTemBo”.

STEP 5: LOCATE THE BUILD CHAMBER SOCKET CONNECTOR

Locate the build chamber socket connector on the bottom-left of the temperature board.

STEP 6: RESEAT THE BUILD CHAMBER SOCKET CONNECTOR

Gently pull on the white connector to remove the build chamber cable bundle from the socket on the board. Wait ten seconds, then reinsert the connector into the board. Ensure that the connector is fully seated into the socket on the board.

NOTICE:  Do not pull on the wires, as this may damage the cable.

STEP 7: CLEAN THE TEMPERATURE BOARD

If there is any powder on the board, gently clean it off with compressed air.

STEP 8: REINSTALL THE LOWER REAR PANEL

Reinstall the back lower rear panel. Take care to place the black screws in the locations marked in blue and the chrome screws in the holes marked in orange.

STEP 9: REINSTALL CONNECTIVITY ACCESSORIES

Reinstall the Wi-Fi antenna. Plug in the USB and Ethernet cables.

STEP 10: PLUG IN THE PRINTER

Plug in and power on the printer.

Finalizing the repair

OVERVIEW: After reinstalling the printer’s rear shell, perform final checks.

STEP 1: INSERT THE BUILD CHAMBER

Insert the build chamber into the Fuse 1. Close the build chamber latch and connect the build chamber plug to the machine.

STEP 2: TEST THE BUILD CHAMBER

Start a print. Contact Formlabs Support or your certified service provider to share the results of the procedure.
