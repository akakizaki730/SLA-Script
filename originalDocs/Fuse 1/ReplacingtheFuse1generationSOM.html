FORMLABS CUSTOMER SUPPORT GUIDES

Replacing the Fuse 1 generation SOM

The system on a module (SOM) plugs into the motherboard and runs the software on the printer. A malfunctioning SOM may cause the printer to freeze during initialization or fail to turn on.

Use this guide to replace the SOM on Fuse 1 generation printers.

Required Supplies:
Formlabs:

  • Replacement Fuse 1 SOM

Third-party:

  • 1.5 mm hex driver
  • 2.5 mm hex driver
  • 3.0 mm 90º hex wrench
  • ESD-safe spudger (recommended) or ESD-safe tweezers
  • Antistatic wrist band
  • Safety glasses
  • Nonreactive nitrile gloves

Estimated time: 60–90 minutes

DANGER: Read and follow all safety instructions and warnings in the operator manual. Replacement with incorrect parts can lead to burns, shock, fire, or explosion. Dispose of all parts in accordance with local regulations.

NOTICE: This procedure involves installing a replacement SOM (system on module) on the Fuse 1 motherboard (also referred to as the Frontal Lobe board or FoLoBo). The motherboard and SOM are sensitive circuit assemblies and must be treated with care. Any damage from mishandling or exposure to static electricity may render the motherboard or SOM, and thus the printer, inoperable.

Preparing the workspace

OVERVIEW: Prepare the printer and a dust-free workspace for replacing the SOM.

NOTICE: Fuse 1 generation printers contain sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using a grounding strap or touching a grounded piece of metal.

STEP 1: PREPARE THE WORKSPACE

Clear a workspace around your printer. Ensure that the workspace is well-ventilated and free of dust.

STEP 2: UNPLUG THE PRINTER

Disconnect the power cable before continuing with maintenance. Do not perform maintenance on the printer while it is connected to power. Wait five minutes after disconnecting the printer from power before proceeding.

DANGER: Performing maintenance on the printer while it is plugged in increases the risk of electric shock. Never remove the back panel of the printer while the printer is plugged in. Never reconnect the printer to power while the back panel is uninstalled.

Removing the top enclosure panel

OVERVIEW: Remove the top enclosure panel to access the interior of the printer.

STEP 1: UNSCREW THE TOP PANEL SCREWS

Six 6 mm buffer side panel screws.

Twelve 6 mm top panel screws.

Eight 12 mm hopper side panel screws.

Using a 2.5 mm hex driver, remove the 26 top panel screws securing the top panel to the printer. (Six 6 mm screws from the buffer side of the printer, twelve 6 mm screws from the top of the printer, and eight 12 mm screws from the hopper side of the printer.) Set the screws aside.

STEP 2: REMOVE THE TOP PANEL FROM THE PRINTER

Lift the top panel off of the printer by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge. Set the top panel aside.

Removing the buffer side enclosure panel (Fuse 1+ 30 W only)

OVERVIEW: Remove the buffer side enclosure panel to access the interior of the printer. If your printer does not have a nitrogen supply, skip to the next section, Removing the upper cooling shroud.

STEP 1: UNSCREW THE BUFFER SIDE ENCLOSURE PANEL SCREWS

Using a 2.5 mm hex driver, remove the four 6 mm screws that secure the buffer side enclosure panel to the printer. Set the screws aside.

STEP 2: REMOVE THE BUFFER SIDE ENCLOSURE PANEL FROM THE PRINTER

Lift the buffer side enclosure panel off the printer. Set the panel aside on a flat, clean surface.

Removing the upper cooling shroud

OVERVIEW: Remove the upper cooling shroud to access the interior of the printer.

NOTICE: The Fuse 1 contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using a grounding strap or touching a grounded piece of metal.

STEP 1: REMOVE THE IR SENSOR

Grip the IR sensor cap and pull. The IR sensor is held in place with magnets and comes out with little force.

STEP 2: UNPLUG THE IR SENSOR ETHERNET CABLE

Unplug the IR sensor ethernet cable. Feed the ethernet cable through the plastic shroud so that it drops into the printer. Set the IR sensor aside on a flat surface with the sensor window facing up. If your printer does not have a nitrogen supply, skip ahead to Step 8: Unscrew the upper cooling shroud.

STEP 3: DISCONNECT THE N2 SENSOR AND VALVE (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Disconnect the N2 sensor and valve from the cable labeled CBL-109131-00. Feed the cable through the plastic shroud so that it drops into the printer.

STEP 4: MARK THE N2 DOWNSTREAM TUBE (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Using a permanent marker, mark the N2 downstream tube to indicate its install depth while connected to the N2 T-splitter.

STEP 5: DECOUPLE THE N2 DOWNSTREAM TUBE (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

While pulling on the N2 downstream tube, push the brass ring down to decouple the tube from the N2 T-splitter.

STEP 6: MARK THE N2 INTAKE TUBE (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Using a permanent marker, mark the N2 intake tube to indicate its install depth while connected to the N2 intake plenum.

STEP 7: DECOUPLE THE N2 INTAKE TUBE (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

While pulling on the N2 intake tube, push the brass ring down to decouple the tube from the N2 intake plenum.

STEP 8: UNSCREW THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD

Using a 3.0 mm hex driver, remove the four screws securing the upper cooling shroud to the printer. Set the screws aside.

NOTICE: Do not remove the upper cooling shroud without disconnecting the attached cables.

STEP 9: LIFT THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD

Gently lift up but do not separate the front edge of the plastic shroud from the top of the printer to access the attached cables. Take care not to pull on the cables, as this may damage them.

STEP 10: DISCONNECT THE FAN CABLE FROM THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD

Disconnect the fan cable labeled CO47 by squeezing the plastic latch and gently pulling the connectors away from each other. Do not pull on the wires.

TIP: Formlabs recommends having one person hold the upper cooling shroud while another person unplugs the cables.

STEP 11: UNPLUG THE RFID READER CABLE FROM THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD (FUSE 1 ONLY)

Grip the plastic connector on the RFID reader cable and pull directly away from the upper cooling shroud. Do not pull on the wires.

STEP 12: REMOVE THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD

Once all the cables are disconnected, lift the upper cooling shroud away from the machine. Set the upper cooling shroud aside.

Removing the display assembly

OVERVIEW: Unscrew and remove the display assembly to access the SOM.

STEP 1: GROUND YOURSELF WITH AN ANTISTATIC WRIST BAND

Put on an antistatic wrist band, then connect it to a known ground point.

If you are near the Fuse Sift’s vacuum, use the collection bin’s grounding cable as a grounding point. Ensure the vacuum is connected to the Fuse Sift and a mains outlet.

NOTICE: The Fuse 1 motherboard and SOM are sensitive circuit assemblies and must be treated with care. Any damage from mishandling or exposure to static electricity may render the motherboard or SOM, and thus the printer, inoperable. Do not ground yourself with any surfaces that are powder-coated or painted.

STEP 2: LOCATE THE MOTHERBOARD

Locate the motherboard (also known as a Frontal Lobe board or FoLoBo) on the side of the printer opposite the hopper. It is mounted to the back of the front display panel, and has visible USB and Ethernet connections.

STEP 3: DISCONNECT THE WI-FI COAXIAL CONNECTORS FROM THE MOTHERBOARD

Using a pair of ESD-safe tweezers, disconnect the two Wi-Fi coaxial connectors. Newer printers may only contain one Wi-Fi coaxial connector. Take care while removing the Wi-Fi coaxial connectors, as they are extremely fragile and may bend or break if improperly handled.

STEP 4: UNPLUG THE NETWORKING CABLES FROM THE MOTHERBOARD

Disconnect the Ethernet and USB cables from the motherboard.

STEP 5: UNPLUG THE REMAINING CABLES FROM THE MOTHERBOARD

Unplug the indicated cables from the motherboard. When unplugging the cables, disengage the locking tab, then grip the plastic connector and pull directly away from the motherboard.

For the Fuse 1, after disconnecting the RFID reader cable, set it aside. All other cables should remain inside the top housing of the printer.

STEP 6: LOCATE THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY SCREWS

Open the filter cabinet door, then manually open the print enclosure by pulling the blue release handle.

STEP 7: REMOVE THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY SCREWS

Using a 3.0 mm hex driver, remove the two display assembly screws holding the display assembly to the top of the printer. The screws are located at the bottom corners of the display assembly. Have a second person hold the display assembly when removing the screws to prevent the display assembly from falling. Set the screws aside.

STEP 8: GENTLY REMOVE THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY

Carefully tilt the display assembly from the printer and lift to remove. If the display assembly catches on something, do not pull it away from the printer. Check to ensure that it is free and clear of the yellow, black, red, and white cable harness above the door handle.

Lay the display assembly with the glass face down on a clean, level surface.

Removing the SOM

OVERVIEW: Unscrew and remove the SOM from the motherboard.

STEP 1: GROUND YOURSELF WITH AN ANTISTATIC WRIST BAND

Put on an antistatic wrist band, then connect it to a known ground point.

If you are near the Fuse Sift’s vacuum, use the collection bin’s grounding cable as a grounding point. Ensure the vacuum is connected to the Fuse Sift and a mains outlet.

NOTICE: The Fuse 1 motherboard and SOM are sensitive circuit assemblies and must be treated with care. Any damage from mishandling or exposure to static electricity may render the motherboard or SOM, and thus the printer, inoperable. Do not ground yourself with any surfaces that are powder-coated or painted.

STEP 2: UNSCREW THE SOM RETENTION SCREWS

Using a 1.5 mm hex driver, remove the two retention screws securing the SOM to the motherboard. Set the screws aside.

STEP 3: LOCATE THE SOM RETENTION TABS

Locate the retention tabs on either side of the SOM securing it to the motherboard.

STEP 4: UNLOCK THE SOM RETENTION TABS

Using an ESD-safe spudger or a pair of ESD-safe tweezers, disengage both retention tabs.

STEP 5: PIVOT THE SOM AWAY FROM THE MOTHERBOARD

Gently grip the SOM by the heat sink and pivot the SOM upwards and away from the motherboard. Do not unplug the SOM by pulling parallel to the motherboard. Instead, keep the connector engaged with the motherboard when pivoting the SOM upwards to avoid damaging the connection interface.

STEP 6: REMOVE THE SOM

While holding the SOM at a 30º angle, gently separate the SOM from the motherboard connector. Set the SOM aside for disposal with electronic waste.

Installing the replacement SOM

OVERVIEW: After removing the SOM, install the replacement SOM.

STEP 1: GROUND YOURSELF WITH AN ANTISTATIC WRIST BAND

Put on an antistatic wrist band, then connect it to a known ground point.

If you are near the Fuse Sift’s vacuum, use the collection bin’s grounding cable as a grounding point. Ensure the vacuum is connected to the Fuse Sift and a mains outlet.

NOTICE: The Fuse 1 motherboard and SOM are sensitive circuit assemblies and must be treated with care. Any damage from mishandling or exposure to static electricity may render the motherboard or SOM, and thus the printer, inoperable. Do not ground yourself with any surfaces that are powder-coated or painted.

STEP 2: INSERT THE SOM INTO THE MOTHERBOARD CONNECTOR

Gently pick up the replacement SOM by the heat sink. While holding the SOM at a 30º angle with the motherboard, insert the connector pins into the connector on the motherboard.

NOTICE: Be careful not to crush or damage the SOM connector pins. Damaging the SOM will prevent the printer from working properly.

Gently push the SOM at a 30º angle into the motherboard connector until it is fully seated in its connector and none of the gold pins on the SOM are visible.

STEP 3: PUSH THE SOM DOWN

Gently push the SOM down onto the motherboard so that it lays flush with the motherboard. Ensure the SOM screw holes are aligned with the corresponding holes in the motherboard assembly.

STEP 4: ENGAGE THE SOM RETENTION TABS

Using an ESD-safe spudger or a pair of ESD-safe tweezers, engage the retention tabs on both sides of the SOM.

STEP 5: SECURE THE SOM TO THE MOTHERBOARD

Using a 1.5 mm hex driver, screw in the two 5 mm SOM retention screws until finger tight.

Reinstalling the display assembly

OVERVIEW: After installing the new SOM, reinstall the display assembly to the printer.

STEP 1: GROUND YOURSELF WITH AN ANTISTATIC WRIST BAND

Put on an antistatic wrist band, then connect it to a known ground point.

If you are near the Fuse Sift’s vacuum, use the collection bin’s grounding cable as a grounding point. Ensure the vacuum is connected to the Fuse Sift and a mains outlet.

NOTICE: The Fuse 1 motherboard and SOM are sensitive circuit assemblies and must be treated with care. Any damage from mishandling or exposure to static electricity may render the motherboard or SOM, and thus the printer, inoperable. Do not ground yourself with any surfaces that are powder-coated or painted.

STEP 2: ALIGN THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY WITH THE PRINTER

With a second person holding the display assembly at an angle, align the tab at the bottom of the display assembly with the gap directly above the print enclosure opening on the printer. Ensure cable harnesses are not crushed by the tabs on the back of the display space. The cable harnesses should sit clear of the space between the two tabs on the back of the display assembly.

STEP 3: PIVOT THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY TO LAY FLUSH AGAINST THE PRINTER

Pivot the display assembly towards the printer so the display assembly’s flange tabs are flush with the corresponding flanges on the frame.

STEP 4: THREAD THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY SCREWS

Thread the two 6 mm display assembly screws into the screw holes on the flanges from the printer side. Do not fully tighten the screws.

STEP 5: CHECK THE SURROUNDING CABLE HARNESSES

Ensure that the cable harnesses above the door handle remain free and clear of the display assembly and the printer frame.

STEP 6: TIGHTEN THE DISPLAY ASSEMBLY SCREWS

Using a 3.0 mm 90º hex wrench, tighten the display assembly screws until finger tight.

STEP 7: RECONNECT MOTHERBOARD CABLES AND CONNECTORS

Begin reconnecting the seven JST connectors, the ethernet cable, the USB cable, and the Wi-Fi coaxial cable to the motherboard. Refer to the image for mapping each connector with its socket.

Reinstalling the upper cooling shroud

OVERVIEW: Reinstall and secure the upper cooling shroud.

STEP 1: RECONNECT THE RFID READER CABLE TO THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD (FUSE 1 ONLY)

With one person holding the upper cooling shroud above the printer, reconnect the RFID reader cable into the white connector. The cable will only enter the connector in the correct orientation. Be careful not to bend the pins in the connector. Do not pull on the wires.

STEP 2: RECONNECT THE FAN CABLE TO THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD

With one person holding the upper cooling shroud above the printer, reconnect the cooling fan cable into the socket labeled C047. The cable will only enter the connector in the correct orientation. Be careful not to bend the pins in the connector.

STEP 3: ALIGN THE COOLING SHROUD ABOVE THE PRINTER

Hold the upper cooling shroud by the front edge and align it above the printer so that you can access the cables covered by the shroud. Feed the IR sensor ethernet cable through the cutout in the top shroud.

STEP 4: SECURE THE UPPER COOLING SHROUD TO THE PRINTER

Thread one screw into each of the four screw holes in the upper cooling shroud. Using a 3.0 mm hex driver, tighten the screws until finger tight. If your printer does not have a nitrogen supply, skip to “Step 9: Inspect the IR sensor lens”.

STEP 5: TUCK THE N2 TUBE UNDERNEATH THE HOPPER SHROUD (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Tuck the N2 tube connected to the T-splitter and N2 valve under the hopper shroud flange.

STEP 6: RECONNECT THE N2 SENSOR AND VALVE (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Reconnect the N2 sensor to the cable labeled CBL-109131-00. Feed the cable back through the cutout in the upper cooling shroud, then reconnect the N2 valve to the cable.

STEP 7: INSERT THE N2 DOWNSTREAM TUBE INTO THE T-SPLITTER (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Insert the N2 downstream tube into the middle fitting of the brass N2 T-splitter. Ensure the depth indicator mark aligns with the fitting’s release ring.

STEP 8: INSERT THE N2 INTAKE TUBE INTO THE INTAKE PLENUM (FUSE 1+ 30W ONLY)

Insert the N2 intake tube into the N2 intake plenum’s brass fitting. Ensure the depth indicator mark aligns with the fitting’s release ring.

STEP 9: INSPECT THE IR SENSOR LENS

Inspect the IR sensor lens for debris. Clean the IR sensor lens with ethanol and PEC*PAD if needed. Check the lens to ensure that there are no streaks and that the contamination has been fully removed.

STEP 10: REINSTALL THE IR SENSOR AND ETHERNET CABLE

Reconnect the ethernet cable to the IR sensor, then push the IR sensor cap into the mount.

Reinstalling the buffer side enclosure panel (FUSE 1+ 30 W only)

OVERVIEW: After replacing the SOM, reinstall the buffer side enclosure panel. If your printer does not have a nitrogen supply, skip to the next section, Reinstalling the top enclosure panel.

STEP 1: ALIGN THE BUFFER SIDE ENCLOSURE PANEL WITH THE PRINTER

Lift the buffer side enclosure panel and align it with the buffer side of the printer.

STEP 2: SECURE THE BUFFER SIDE ENCLOSURE PANEL

Thread the four 6.00 mm screws into the buffer side of the printer. Using a 2.5 mm hex driver, tighten the screws until snug.

Reinstalling the top enclosure panel

OVERVIEW: After performing any other maintenance procedures, reinstall and secure the top enclosure panel.

STEP 1: ALIGN THE TOP ENCLOSURE PANEL WITH THE PRINTER

Lift the top enclosure panel by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge, then align it with the top of the printer.

STEP 2: SECURE THE UPPER ENCLOSURE PANEL

Thread the six 6 mm screws into the screw holes on the buffer side of the printer, the twelve 6 mm screws into the top of the printer, and the eight 12 mm screws into the hopper side of the printer. Using a 2.5 mm hex driver, tighten the screws until snug.

Finalizing the repair

OVERVIEW: After installing the new SOM, power on the printer and perform final checks.

STEP 1: POWER ON THE PRINTER

Close the printer cover and connect the power cable to the printer and the power supply. The printer initializes and displays a series of onboarding steps on the touchscreen. Follow these steps to prepare the printer for use.

STEP 2: UPDATE THE PRINTER’S FIRMWARE

Update the printer’s firmware. Test the printer to ensure that it operates normally. Contact Formlabs Support or your authorized reseller to share the results of the procedure.
